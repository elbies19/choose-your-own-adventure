Perusall

Collaborative annotation that makes reading social

The Challenge

Students aren't doing the reading — or they're skimming it without really engaging. You end up re-teaching everything in class anyway, and discussions fall flat because nobody came prepared.

How This Helps

Perusall turns reading into a conversation. Students annotate assigned texts together, ask questions in the margins, and respond to each other — all before they walk into your classroom. You can see exactly who engaged, what confused them, and what sparked their interest. It shifts reading from a solo chore to a collaborative activity, and gives you a window into student thinking before class even starts.

DesignPLUS

Professional course design without needing to be a designer

The Challenge

You want your Canvas course to look polished and easy to navigate, but you're not a graphic designer and the default Canvas editor only gets you so far. You see other courses that look great and wonder how they pulled it off.

How This Helps

DesignPLUS is a browser extension that adds a suite of design tools right inside your Canvas editor. GPTZero

AI literacy tools built for educators, not against students

The Challenge

AI is here and your students are using it — but you're not sure how to tell when it's being used inappropriately, and you don't want to become the AI police. You need a tool that helps you navigate this thoughtfully, not one that turns every assignment into a courtroom.

How This Helps

GPTZero takes an education-first approach to AI and academic integrity. It includes detection capabilities, but more importantly, it offers tools like writing process tracking and citation verification that help you understand how students are working — not just flag them for suspicion. It's designed to support teaching and learning conversations about AI, not shut them down.

Canvas Studio

Create, share, and track video — all inside Canvas

The Challenge

You'd love to use more video in your teaching — screen recordings, mini-lectures, instructional walkthroughs — but you're not sure where to start, and uploading to YouTube feels clunky. More importantly, you want to know if students actually watched what you created.

How This Helps

Canvas Studio lets you record, upload, and share video directly inside Canvas — no separate platform needed. You can create screen recordings with your webcam overlay, embed videos in any Canvas page or assignment, and add inline quiz questions to keep students engaged. The real game-changer is the analytics: you can see exactly which students watched, how much they watched, and where they dropped off. That's powerful information for follow-up and accountability.

UDOIT

Make your course work better for every student

The Challenge

You want to make sure all of your students can access and use your course materials — including students with visual, hearing, motor, or cognitive disabilities. But accessibility guidelines feel overwhelming, and you're not sure where to start or what you might be missing.

How This Helps

UDOIT scans your entire Canvas course and flags accessibility issues — missing alt text on images, low color contrast, improperly structured headings, and more. It doesn't just tell you what's wrong; in many cases, it lets you fix the issue right from within the tool. Think of it as a spell-checker, but for accessibility. Making your course more accessible isn't just about compliance — it's about Universal Design for Learning, which means making your materials clearer and more usable for everyone.

New Quizzes

Assessments that do more than multiple choice

The Challenge

Classic Quizzes in Canvas are fine for basic assessments, but they're limited. You want more question types, better options for randomization, and assessments that actually test understanding — not just recall.

How This Helps

New Quizzes is Canvas's updated assessment engine with a wider range of question types — hotspot, drag-and-drop, categorization, ordering, stimulus-based questions, and more. It offers better item banking, richer moderation tools, and stronger options for building assessments that ask students to think rather than just remember. If you've been frustrated by the limitations of Classic Quizzes, this is the upgrade you've been waiting for.

Enhanced Rubrics

Rubrics that work harder so you don't have to

The Challenge

You know rubrics are important for consistent grading, but the old rubric tool in Canvas felt clunky and limited. And when it comes time for outcomes assessment and Closing the Loop reporting, pulling rubric data is a headache you dread every semester.

How This Helps

Enhanced Rubrics is Canvas's redesigned rubric creation tool with a more intuitive interface, better alignment options, and improved integration with outcomes assessment workflows. Building rubrics is faster and more flexible, and the connection between rubric criteria and learning outcomes is cleaner — which means less manual work when it's time to pull data for your assessment reports. Better rubrics up front means less pain at reporting time.

AI Literacy Training Course

Learn at your own pace — no judgment, no jargon

The Challenge

AI is everywhere in the news and your students are using tools like ChatGPT — but you haven't had time (or a clear reason) to sit down and really understand what it is, how it works, and what it means for your teaching. You're not against it; you just don't know where to begin, and most of what you've seen is either too technical or too breathless.

How This Helps

This self-paced Canvas course walks you through AI from the ground up — what it is, what it can and can't do, how your students are already using it, and how you might thoughtfully incorporate it into your own teaching. It's organized into four modules: Learn, Read, Practice, and Teach. The goal isn't to make you an AI expert overnight — it's to give you enough understanding to make informed decisions about your own classroom. No hype, no panic, just practical knowledge you can use.
